EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
On February 26, 2026, a sustained SSH brute-force attack was detected against endpoint ubuntu12 (10.128.124.88), originating from 10.128.124.9 (Kali Linux — simulated threat actor). The attack resulted in 1,391+ failed authentication attempts targeting the root account over 15 minutes. The attack was detected within 2 minutes by Splunk correlation rule SOC-001, automatically triaged by Shuffle SOAR, enriched via VirusTotal API, and a Jira incident ticket (SOC-2) was auto-created within 45 seconds of detection. 
- SEVERITY: HIGH 
- STATUS: RESOLVED 
- DURATION: 15 minutes (attack) / 45 min (full IR)

- 15 SPL correlation rules active and tuned.

- All systems confirmed operational

LESSONS LEARNED

What Worked Well:

✓ Detection time under 2 minutes
✓ SOAR reduced manual response by 90%
✓ Jira ticket auto-created in 45 seconds
✓ MITRE mapping identified the full attack chain
✓ Threat hunting found additional IOCs

What Needs Improvement:

- SSH port should move to a non-standard port
- fail2ban should be installed on all endpoints
- VirusTotal check needs public IP handling
- Alert fatigue — 1391 events from one attack

Recommendations:

1. Implement fail2ban on all Linux endpoints
2. Disable root SSH login permanently
3. Add GeoIP blocking for non-India IPs
4. Increase Splunk index retention to 90 days
5. Schedule weekly threat hunting reviews
